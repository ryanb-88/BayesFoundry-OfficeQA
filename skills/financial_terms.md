# Financial Terms Glossary for Treasury Bulletins

## Debt Terms
- **Gross Federal Debt**: Total outstanding debt including intragovernmental holdings
- **Debt Held by Public**: Gross debt minus intragovernmental holdings
- **Statutory Debt Limit**: Legal ceiling on total federal debt
- **Par Value**: Face value of a security (not market value)
- **Accrued Interest**: Interest earned but not yet paid

## Budget Terms
- **Receipts**: Government income from taxes, duties, fees, etc.
- **Outlays**: Government spending/expenditures
- **Surplus**: Receipts exceed outlays (positive value)
- **Deficit**: Outlays exceed receipts (negative value, sometimes shown in parentheses)
- **Budget Authority**: Legal authority to incur financial obligations
- **Appropriations**: Congressional authorization of funds
- **Off-budget**: Items excluded from unified budget totals (e.g., Social Security)

## Securities Terms
- **Marketable Securities**: Publicly traded (bills, notes, bonds)
- **Nonmarketable Securities**: Not publicly traded (savings bonds, state/local series)
- **Treasury Bills**: Short-term (≤1 year), sold at discount
- **Treasury Notes**: Medium-term (2–10 years), pay semiannual coupon
- **Treasury Bonds**: Long-term (>10 years), pay semiannual coupon

## Government Structure Terms
- **Trust Funds**: Accounts for specific programs (Social Security, Medicare, highways)
- **Intergovernmental**: Transactions between federal and state/local governments
- **Federal Funds**: All accounts not designated as trust funds
- **Unified Budget**: Combined on-budget and off-budget totals

## International Terms
- **Liabilities to Foreigners**: What the U.S. owes to foreign entities
- **Claims on Foreigners**: What foreign entities owe to the U.S.
- **Net International Investment Position**: Claims minus liabilities

## Mathematical Operations
- When asked for "total": Sum all listed items
- When asked for "net": Difference between two categories
- When asked for "ratio": Divide numerator by denominator
- When asked for "percentage change": ((new - old) / old) × 100
- When asked for "regression": Use the specified model type on the given data range
