# U.S. Treasury Bulletin Reference Guide

## Document Structure
- Published monthly (1939–1995), then quarterly (1996–2025)
- Each bulletin: 100–200 pages of tables, charts, and narrative
- Total corpus: ~89,000 pages across 8+ decades
- Pre-1996: scanned physical documents (OCR may be unreliable)
- Post-1996: digitally produced PDFs (better text extraction)

## Common Table Types
- Budget receipts and expenditures (by department/agency)
- Public debt operations and outstanding debt
- Federal securities (issued, retired, outstanding)
- International financial statistics
- Liabilities to / claims on foreigners
- Trust fund operations

## Date Conventions — CRITICAL
- Calendar Year (CY): January 1 – December 31
- Fiscal Year (FY post-1977): October 1 – September 30
- Fiscal Year (FY pre-1977): July 1 – June 30
- ALWAYS check whether a question asks for CY or FY — using the wrong one is a common error
- Monthly data is labeled by month/year
- Some tables show both CY and FY — verify which column you are reading

## Units — Always Verify
- Most tables report values in MILLIONS of dollars
- Some tables use thousands or billions — check column headers
- Percentages are typically decimal values (5.2 means 5.2%)
- Some tables use parentheses () for negative values
- "n.a." or "—" typically means data not available

## Navigating the Bulletins
- Table of Contents appears at the front of each bulletin
- Tables are organized by subject area
- Cross-references between tables are common
- Data revisions appear in later bulletins — use the issue specified in the question

## Report Pages
- Some questions reference specific "report pages" — this means the page number printed on the document, NOT the PDF page number. These may differ due to front matter.
